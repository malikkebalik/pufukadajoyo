Charles McGonigal Arrested For Having Ties With Russian Oligarch Oleg Deripaska

Charles ‘Charlie’ McGonigal, aka Charles McGonigal (born in 1968, Age: 54 years old), is a retired FBI agent who served 18 years as a federal employee. Following his service as an investigator, he started offering his services in the private sector and worked as Vice President of Brookfield Properties till January 2023.

Recently the former top FBI official has become a subject of headlines because he got arrested against severe charges of violating Russian sanctions and money laundering. According to MSNBC, Charles is facing five counts related to business dealings after he left the FBI in 2018.

The word is that McGonigal helped Putin’s ally, Oleg evade U.S. sanctions on Russia and also accepted $225,000 from an Albanian intelligence official while he was serving in the FBI as a top counterintelligence agent.

Experts are describing an event as a significant blow to the FBI’s reputation, as an official whose role was to protect the United States and catch spies has been revealed to be working for a Russian oligarch.

Charles McGonigal’s Career

Mr. McGonigal is an experienced professional with a track record of success in counterintelligence and cyber threat management. He has extensive expertise in managing and conducting espionage, economic espionage, and insider threat investigations.

He has led investigations on some of the most high-profile espionage cases in the U.S., resulting in the conviction of prominent individuals such as former National Security Advisor Samuel Berger.

He has also played a key role in national security investigations in partnership with the Department of Justice and the Department of Treasury, resulting in over $1 billion in fines for global financial institutions such as ING and HSBC for violations of the IEEP Act.

In 2010, he was appointed to lead the FBI’s WikiLeaks Task Force, which led to the identification of Pfc. Bradley Manning of severe violations related to espionage.

In October 2016, the then FBI director James B. Comey appointed him as the special agent in charge of counterintelligence, but he held that position for only two years retiring in 2018.
