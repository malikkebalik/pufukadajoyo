Farewell to Andrea Evans One Life to Live and The Young and the Restless Star at 66

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, September 19, 2024

The passing of renowned actress Andrea Evans has left the entertainment industry in mourning. Evans played a pivotal part in the long-running soap opera “The Young and the Restless.” Evans won the hearts of millions of people all over the world with her outstanding skill, magnetic charisma, and unforgettable performances.

The tragic news of her passing stunned admirers and coworkers alike, and will never be filled. We honor the life and legacy of a true television star by remembering her remarkable career and the profound impression she left on the industry. Know all details about Andrea Evans’s death below.

Andrea Evans Death

Ms. Evans passed away at the age of 66. Former manager Don Carroll told that Evans passed away on Sunday due to cancer. Her current manager Nick Leicht said I’ve been working with Andrea for the past 7 years. She was a phenomenal performer and a pleasure to collaborate with.

Evans’ breakout roles as troubled teen Tina Lord on One Life to Live and as Patty Williams on The Young and the Restless made her a household name. In 1988, for her role as Tina, she was nominated for a Daytime Emmy Award for outstanding ingenue in a drama series.

“Andrea was a kind woman, fun to work with, and such a pro,” Archer wrote on Facebook. “I feel so fortunate that I was able to play with her on OLTL and The Bay the Series.”

“People often think she must be very like the characters she played on television,” Carroll remarked of the actress. In reality, her greatest days were spent with no makeup, her hair in pigtails, and a ballcap on her head while visiting Disneyland with her daughter Kylie.

Former One Life to Live EP and current General Hospital EP Frank Valentini tweeted his condolences, saying, I am so sorry to learn of Andrea Evans’ loss. When I first started working at OLTL, Andrea was already a tremendous star.
